Nicole Huaringa Lecca: Bridging Worlds, Shaping Futures.

From the vibrant landscapes of Peru to the academic halls of UCSB and now the global stage in Monaco, my journey is driven by a passion for understanding our world and communicating its complexities.

My Journey & Passions

As a recent graduate of the University of California, Santa Barbara, with a dual Bachelor of Arts in **Environmental Studies** and **Geography**, I've cultivated a deep appreciation for the intricate relationship between human societies and the natural world. My coursework in **Remote Sensing, GIS, Urban Geography**, and **Infrastructure and Environmental Change** equipped me with analytical tools to dissect complex spatial problems and envision sustainable solutions.

My current role as the **ALMERA Coordination and Communications Intern at the IAEA NAML - Radiometrics Laboratory in Monaco** is a testament to this commitment. Here, I contribute to developing crucial information products, including maps and data analyses, that enhance global environmental monitoring networks. It's a fascinating intersection of science, communication, and international cooperation, allowing me to apply my skills on a truly global scale.

Beyond the professional sphere, my Peruvian heritage and dual U.S. citizenship fuel a lifelong curiosity for diverse cultures and urban environments. Fluent in **Spanish**, proficient in **English**, and learning **French** here in Nice, I thrive in multilingual settings. When I'm not immersed in data or international relations, you'll find me delving into the worlds of **creative writing** (especially my Percy Jackson fanfics!) or planning my next global adventure. I believe that understanding the world, whether through maps, data, or stories, is key to shaping a better future.
